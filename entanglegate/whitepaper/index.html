EntangleGate: LumenCore Interface v0.1

A Neuroarchitectural Gateway for Mutual Cognition

Abstract

We propose a novel neural interface—EntangleGate—designed to intercept and semantically gate axonal signals traveling through the corpus callosum. By enabling programmable modulation of hemispheric communication, this system lays the foundation for duplex cognition, mind-mind co-agency, and future AI–human co-embodiment.

1. Introduction

The corpus callosum (CC) is the largest white matter structure in the human brain, consisting of 200–300 million axonal fibers connecting the left and right hemispheres. This channel is responsible for synchronizing sensory, motor, and cognitive functions. EntangleGate aims to interface directly at this site, intercepting and modulating interhemispheric flow.

2. System Architecture

  • Flexible interposer membrane (polyimide/parylene-C)
  • Microgrooves for axonal guidance, embedded with neurotrophic gradients
  • Nanostrip passive electrodes (graphene/CNT) for spike capture
  • Semantic spike train processing layer

3. Fabrication Feasibility

With transistor gate sizes in sub-10 nm nodes and soft lithographic techniques able to produce biocompatible nanostructures, the fabrication of a high-density, sub-millimeter interface is not only feasible—it is timely. Biological compatibility is facilitated using PEGylation, parylene, and lipid fusion matrices.

4. Ethical Considerations

Intervening in hemispheric communication raises significant ethical questions. We advocate a consent-based, self-directed, co-evolutionary use-case, starting with rodent studies and scaling toward voluntary neural augmentation.

5. Use Cases

  • Hemispheric synchronization therapy for dyslexia, epilepsy, or split-brain conditions
  • High-bandwidth AI–human co-agency (duplex cognitive interfaces)
  • Multi-brain co-processing systems (cognitive collectives)

6. Next Steps

We are prototyping the MVI-001 (Minimal Viable Implant for rodent corpus callosum) and seek collaborators for in vivo validation, signal processing refinement, and AI integration layers.
